Impact of tides on non-coplanar orbits of progenitors of high-mass X-ray binaries

An interesting topic regarding the role of tides (like the ones acting on the oceans due to the moon and the earth not being a solid body) on binaries containing a common-type star and a compact-object (neutron star or black hole). it turns out, they are very important in predicting a lot of stellar (and binary) parameters !

A GitHub repository contains all the source code to produce the raw data as well as scripts to reproduce all the figures in the manuscript. I strongly support a reproducible science approach
